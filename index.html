About Me

Research Profile & Summary

Lecturer and early-career researcher in Computer Science & Engineering with a focused research agenda in Computer Vision, Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), and Large Language Models (LLMs). Developed and evaluated deep learning systems for visual understanding across medical imaging, agricultural diagnostics, and intelligent sensing applications, involving data from X-ray, CT, MRI, retinal, microscopic, and thermal modalities. Research contributions emphasize lightweight and hybrid CNN–Vision Transformer architectures, attention mechanisms, and explainable AI techniques (e.g., Grad-CAM) for robust and interpretable visual recognition. Published 52 peer-reviewed papers and co-authored a Bangla ML/DL book. Seeking a PhD position to pursue rigorous research on advanced vision and vision–language architectures, graph-structured learning, and interpretable AI systems with real-world impact.
